Present Courses

  • Environmental Sciences M.Sc.

    • ENVC 24: Energy and Environment

      [This postgraduate course discusses on the science of energy scales and their relative implications on the environment. This includes basics of conventional and nonconventional sources, energy management/policies and energy security of Earth.]

  • Physics Honours (PHSH)

    • Paper IIA (Unit I): Thermal Physics I

      [This is an UG course on equilibrium/nonequilibrium physics with a focus on "Heat", without the use of Statistical physics. Topics include basic assumptions of kinetic theory and derivation of gas laws, velocity/energy distribution, atomic mean free path, transport phenomena & Brownian motion, Real gases and Virial, Heat conduction, Blackbody radiation].

    • Paper IVA (Unit I): Thermal Physics II

      [This is an UG course on non-equilibrium/nearly-equilibrium physics with a focus on "Thermodynamics", without the use of Statistical physics. This includes basic concepts of Thermodynamics, zeroth, first, second and third law of Thermodynamics, Thermodynamic potentials and Phase Equilibria].

    • Paper VI (Unit II): Solid State Physics I

      [This is an UG course on condensed matter physics with a spotlight on crystalline and amorphous solids. This includes distinction between states of matter, crystal structure, lattice and basis, lattice planes, Laue and Bragg law, X-ray diffraction, crystal bonding, Free electron theory and band theory of solids, conductors, semiconductors, insulators, metals].

    • Paper VIIIB: Computer Laboratory

      [This is an UG course that describes on numerical mathematics/computational physics in high level language C. It includes basic coding and performing arithmatic operations and solving various algebraic equations in different geometry, finding roots of a quadratic equation, sorting and {mean,median,mode} of a number system, matrix addition and multiplication, finding prime numbers on a given range, Gauss-Siedel iterative solvers, Roots by Newton-Raphson method, Lagrange Interpolation formula, Trapezoidal and Simpson's Rule of integration].

  • Physics General (PHSG)

    • Paper IB: Waves and Oscillations

      [This is an UG course that discuss on the physics of Waves and Vibrations. This includes basics of SHM, superposition and Lissajous plots, free and forced damped SHM and various resonances, Wave equation in different geometry, Doppler effect].

    • Paper IVA: Communications

      [This is an UG course that discuss on the physics of Communications. It motivates the subject by propagation of EM wave, transmission of such waves through media, modulation and demodulation].

    • Paper IVB: Computer Laboratory

      [This is an UG course that teaches about numerical mathematics/computational physics in high level language C. It includes basic coding and performing arithmatic operations and solving various algebraic equations from computing area and volume of different geometry, finding roots of a quadratic equation, bubble sorting and mean/median, matrix addition and substraction].
