Present Courses

  • Physics Honours (PHSH)

    • Paper IIA (Unit I): Thermal Physics I

      [This course is a UG course on equilibrium/nonequilibrium physics with a focus on heat, without the use of statistical physics. This includes basic assumptions of kinetic theory, gas laws, velocity/energy distribution, mean free path, transport phenomena & brownian motion, real gases and virial, heat conduction and blackbody radiation].

    • Paper IV A (Unit I): Thermal Physics II

      [This course is an undergraduate course with a focus on equilibrium and nonequilibrium physics with a particular focus on thermodynamics, without the use of statistical physics. This includes basic concepts of thermodynamics, zeroth, first and second law of thermodynamics, thermodynamics functions and phase equilibria].

    • Paper VI (Unit II): Solid State Physics I

      [This course is an undergraduate course with a focus on condensed matter physics with a particular focus on solids. This includes states of matter with exotic matter, crystal structure, lattice and basis, different lattice planes, Laue and Bragg law, X-ray diffraction, types of bonding, band theory of solids, conductors, semiconductors, insulators, metals and applications].

    • Paper VIIIB: Computer Laboratory

      [This course is an undergraduate honours course that teaches about numerical analysis and computational physics in high level language C. It includes basic coding and performing arithmatic operations and solving various algebraic equations from computing area and volume of different geometry, finding roots of a quadratic equation, bubble sorting and mean/median, matrix addition and substraction, Gauss-Siedel method, Newton-Raphson, Interpolation, Trapezoidal and Simpson's Rule etc].
  • Physics General (PHSG)

    • Paper IB: Waves and Oscillations

      [This course is a undergraduate pass course that discuss on the physics of waves and vibrations. This includes basics of SHM, superposition of SHM, free and forced damped SHM and various resonance, equations for wave motion and waves in different geometry with a finishing touch on Doppler effect].

    • Paper IVA: Communications

      [This course is an undergraduate pass course that discuss on the physics of communications. It motivates the subject by propagation of electromagnetic waves, transmission of such waves through media, modulation and demodulation and so on].

    • Paper IVB: Computer Laboratory

      [This course is an undergraduate pass course that teaches about numerical analysis and computational physics in high level language C. It includes basic coding and performing arithmatic operations and solving various algebraic equations from computing area and volume of different geometry, finding roots of a quadratic equation, bubble sorting and mean/median, matrix addition and substraction etc].
