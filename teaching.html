Thermal Physics I

This course is a undergraduate course with a focus on equilibrium and nonequilibrium physics with a particular focus on heat, without the use of statistical physics. This includes basic assumptions of kinetic theory, perfect gas laws, velocity and energy distribution, mean free path, transport coefficients, brownian motion, intermolecular interactions, equation of state, virial expansion, heat conduction and solution in curvilinear coordinates and blackbody radiation.

Thermal Physics II

This course is a undergraduate course with a focus on equilibrium and nonequilibrium physics with a particular focus on thermodynamics, without the use of statistical physics. This includes basic concepts of thermodynamics, zeroth, first and second law of thermodynamics, thermodynamics functions and phase equilibria.

Solid State Physics I

This course is a undergraduate course with a focus on condensed matter physics with a particular focus on solids. This includes state of matter with exotic matter, crystal structure, lattice and basis, different lattice planes, Laue and Bragg law, X ray diffraction, types of bonding, band theory of solids, conductors, semiconductors, insulators, metals and applications.

Waves and Oscillations

This course is a undergraduate pass course that discuss on the physics of waves and vibrations. This includes basics of SHM, superposition of SHM and equations for wave motion.

Communications

This course is a undergraduate pass course that discuss on the physics of communications. It motivates the subject by propagation of electromagnetic waves, transmission of such waves through media and so forth.
