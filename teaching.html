Present Courses

  • Physics Honours (Semester System UG)

    1. Semester I

      • PHS-A-CC-1-2-TH (Mechanics) [The course introduces GPM with an emphasis on flow properties of viscous fluid. Topics include Couette and Poiseuille flow, hydrostatics. It also introduces non-intertial frames by contemplating Galilean relativity, pseudo forces, gyrating frames & Foucault's pendulum].

      • PHS-A-CC-1-1-P [Mathematical Physics I (Practical)] [This is an introductory course on "Numerical Mathematics" using Python. It includes basics of arithmatic operations, roots of quadratic equations, sorting, statistics, matrix operations & linear curve fitting, plot using Gnuplot].

    2. Semester II

      • PHS-A-CC-2-4-TH (Diffraction) [This course unfolds on basic principles of Diffraction by discussing on Fresnel and Fraunhofer diffraction].

    3. Semester III

      • PHS-A-CC-3-6-TH (Thermodynamics) [This course introduces nearly-equilibrium and equilibrium thermodynamics. Various TD laws motivates the subject with an extension towards TD potentials. After discussing low temperature physics, the course concludes with phase equilibria].

      • PHS-A-CC-3-5-P [Mathematical Physics II (Practical)] [This course elaborates on Numpy, Scipy and Matplotlib. It includes solving (coupled) linear equations of various order, constructing special functions, root-finding, interpolation, differentiation and integration, explicit time-steppers and 3D graph plotting].

    4. Semester IV

      • PHS-A-CC-4-8-TH (Special Theory of Relativity) [This course introduces special relativity, invariance and transformation rules, 4-vectors, homogeneous Lorentz group and Minkowski force].

      • PHS-A-CC-4-8-P [Mathematical Physics III (Practical)] [This course illustrates using Python on solution of ODEs,PDEs & BVPs, Fourier Series, Gaussian & Dirac-delta function, Complex Integral & Roots, Integral transform, Special functions].
      • PHS-A-DSE-A2-TH [Quantum Mechanics (Practical)] [This course illustrates on using Python on solving problems of quantum mechanics. It uses Shooting method to solve TISE and TDSE].

    5. Semester V

      • PHS-A-CC-5-12-TH (Solid State Physics) [This course introduces Condensed Matter Physics. After briefing on Crystal/amorphous structure and X-ray diffraction, the course unfolds into crystal bonding, lattice vibrations, Free-electron theory and concludes with Band theory of solids.]

      • PHS-A-DSE-A2-TH (Holography & Nonlinear Optics) [This course discusses on principles of point source hologram. After that it introduces on nonlinear optical medium, classical anharmonic oscillator.]

    6. Semester VI

      • PHS-A-DSE-A2-TH (Nonlinear Dynamics) [This course introduces on dynamical systems, stability analysis and period two orbit.]

      • PHS-A-DSE-B2-TH (Non-equilibrium Statistical Mechanics) [This course invites on the non-equilibrium notion in Statphy through the fluctuations in correlations and Brownian walker.]

      • PHS-A-CC-5-12-P [Statistical Mechanics (Practical)] [This course illustrates on using Python on handling Random Variables, Scaling exponents, Random Walk & Monte Carlo Technique].

  • Physics General (Semester System UG)

    1. Semester I: PHS-G-CC-1-1-TH (Oscillations & Elasticity) [This course discusses on the physics of Oscillations & theory of elasticity. This includes Simple Harmonic Motion, Superposition of SHM, forced damped SHM, Hooke's law, Torsion of Cylinder, Bending of beam, Twisting a wire etc].

    2. Semester II: PHS-G-CC-2-2-TH (Induction & Maxwell's Equation) [This course unfolds in discussing Faraday's discoveries & Maxwell's equations for EM wave propagation. It lectures on motional emf, self & mutual inductance, necessity for displacement current & Poynting's vector, Polarization].

    3. Semester V: PHS-G-DSE-A-TH (Modern Physics - Special Theory of Relativity) [This course introduces Special Relativity, invariance and transformation rules].
